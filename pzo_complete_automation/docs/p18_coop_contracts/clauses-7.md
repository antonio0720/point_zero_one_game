# Co-op Contracts - Clauses 7

## 7.1 Termination Clause

The co-op contract may be terminated by either party with a written notice, subject to the following conditions:

1. Either party may terminate the contract without cause after providing a notice period of 30 days.
2. In case of breach of contract by one party, the other party has the right to terminate the contract immediately upon delivering a written notice detailing the breach.
3. Upon termination, all obligations and responsibilities under this contract will cease, except for those related to indemnification, confidentiality, and any remaining performance obligations.

## 7.2 Notice of Termination

Any notice of termination shall be delivered in writing by email or certified mail. The effective date of termination will be the date specified within the notice, which shall not be earlier than the end of the notice period.

## 7.3 Consequences of Termination

Upon termination, each party will promptly return to the other all property, documents, and materials belonging to the other party that are in their possession or control, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

## 7.4 Survival Clauses

- Clause 5 (Confidentiality)
- Clause 6 (Indemnification)

## 7.5 Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This contract shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the jurisdiction specified in the Agreement, without giving effect to any principles of conflicts of law. Any disputes arising out of or relating to this agreement will be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts located in the specified jurisdiction.

## 7.6 Entire Agreement

This Co-op Contract constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions, whether written or oral, relating to the subject matter hereof. This contract may not be amended except in writing signed by both parties.

## 7.7 Counterparts

This Co-op Contract may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, but all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Faxed or scanned copies of counterpart signatures shall be considered valid and binding signatures.
