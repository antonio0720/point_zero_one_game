# GDPR-Right-to-Delete-14 (Article 17)

## Overview

The Right to Erasure, also known as the "right to be forgotten," is a key data subject right under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). This article outlines the process for implementing Data Retention and Deletion Automation in compliance with GDPR Article 17.

## Scope

This document applies to all personal data processed by [Your Organization Name], and covers the steps required to implement an automated system for managing Right-to-Erase requests, ensuring compliance with GDPR Article 17.

## Key Concepts

1. **Right to Erasure**: Individuals have the right to have their personal data erased under specific conditions.
2. **Data Retention Policy**: A policy that outlines the time period for which personal data will be stored and when it should be deleted or anonymized.
3. **Deletion Automation**: The use of automated tools to execute Right-to-Erase requests in a timely manner, ensuring consistency and efficiency.
4. **Data Subject Request**: A formal request made by the data subject for exercising their rights under GDPR.

## Implementing Data Retention and Deletion Automation

1. **Develop Data Retention Policy**: Establish a comprehensive Data Retention Policy that outlines the criteria for retaining personal data, as well as the process for deletion or anonymization.
2. **Identify Personal Data Sources**: Map and identify all sources of personal data within your organization to ensure complete coverage in the deletion automation process.
3. **Implement Deletion Automation Mechanisms**: Develop and implement automated tools that can identify, locate, and delete personal data based on the criteria outlined in the Data Retention Policy.
4. **Monitor Compliance**: Regularly review and monitor the effectiveness of your deletion automation mechanisms to ensure ongoing compliance with GDPR Article 17.
5. **Handle Data Subject Requests**: Establish a process for handling data subject requests related to the Right-to-Erase, ensuring prompt and efficient execution of these requests in accordance with GDPR requirements.
6. **Document Procedures**: Maintain detailed documentation of all procedures related to Data Retention and Deletion Automation to facilitate audits and ensure compliance.
7. **Staff Training**: Provide training to staff on the importance of data protection, the Right-to-Erase, and their role in ensuring compliance with GDPR Article 17.
