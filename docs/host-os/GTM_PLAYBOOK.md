# Point Zero One Digital - GTM Playbook for Host OS (Week 1 - 4)

## Overview

This playbook outlines the Go-to-Market (GTM) strategy for our Host Operating System (Host OS), detailing key actions and success metrics for each week of the initial launch phase.

## Non-Negotiables

1. Strict adherence to TypeScript, with 'any' never used. All code is strict-mode.
2. Deterministic effects in all aspects of the game and infrastructure.
3. Production-grade, deployment-ready infrastructure.
4. Focus on delivering a high-quality user experience and gathering valuable feedback.

## Implementation Spec (Week 1 - 4)

### Week 1: Seed 10 Hosts

#### Actions

1. Distribute 10 early access keys to select influencers, streamers, and content creators.
2. Provide a comprehensive onboarding guide for each recipient, detailing gameplay mechanics, system requirements, and troubleshooting tips.
3. Schedule follow-up check-ins with key influencers to gather initial feedback and address any issues.

#### Success Metric

10 active users playing the game within the first week of release.

### Week 2: Collect 3 Clip Examples

#### Actions

1. Encourage early adopters to record and share their gameplay clips on social media platforms.
2. Collaborate with influencers to create engaging content showcasing the unique features of our Host OS.
3. Monitor social media channels for user-generated content, curating and reposting the most compelling clips.

#### Success Metric

At least 3 high-quality gameplay clips shared on popular social media platforms.

### Week 3: Social Proof Loop

#### Actions

1. Leverage user-generated content to create promotional materials, such as trailers, testimonials, and case studies.
2. Collaborate with influencers to host live streams featuring our Host OS, encouraging viewers to join the waitlist.
3. Utilize social media advertising to target potential users based on their interests and interactions with our content.

#### Success Metric

A significant increase in waitlist sign-ups as a result of the social proof campaign.

### Week 4: v2 Waitlist Push

#### Actions

1. Announce the upcoming release of version 2, highlighting new features and improvements based on user feedback.
2. Offer early access to waitlisted users, incentivizing them to share the news with their networks.
3. Launch a targeted email campaign to remind waitlisted users about the impending release and encourage them to invite friends to join the waitlist.

#### Success Metric

A substantial increase in waitlist sign-ups leading up to the release of version 2.

## Edge Cases

1. In case of a significant number of technical issues reported during Week 1, prioritize addressing these concerns before proceeding with other actions.
2. If user-generated content does not meet quality standards, work closely with influencers to improve the content or find alternative creators.
3. Adjust advertising budget based on performance and return on investment (ROI) during Week 4.
