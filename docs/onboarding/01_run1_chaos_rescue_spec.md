# Point Zero One Digital - Run 1 Spec: Chaos Rescue

## Overview

This document outlines the specifications for Run 1 of the game, "Chaos Rescue". The focus is on a guest-first entry, single template choice, curated early pressure (FUBAR moment), one stabilizer lever, and a supportive end screen.

## Non-Negotiables

1. Guest-First Entry: Ensure the game is accessible and engaging for new players.
2. Single Template Choice: Limit the initial choices to simplify the learning curve.
3. Curated Early Pressure (FUBAR Moment): Introduce a challenging situation early on to create tension and encourage player engagement.
4. One Stabilizer Lever: Provide one key mechanism for players to recover from the FUBAR moment and regain control of the game.
5. Supportive End Screen: Offer helpful resources or suggestions for improvement after the game, fostering a positive user experience.

## Implementation Spec

1. **Guest-First Entry**: Design an intuitive onboarding process that guides new players through the basics of the game. This includes tutorials, tips, and clear instructions.

2. **Single Template Choice**: Offer a preselected template for the initial game setup. This template should be balanced and engaging to ensure a positive first impression.

3. **Curated Early Pressure (FUBAR Moment)**: Implement a challenging situation early in the game that requires quick thinking and strategic decision-making from the player. This could include financial crises, market volatility, or unexpected events.

4. **One Stabilizer Lever**: Provide a mechanism for players to recover from the FUBAR moment. This could be a powerful asset, a strategic move, or a unique opportunity that becomes available at this critical juncture.

5. **Supportive End Screen**: Display helpful resources or suggestions for improvement after the game. This could include links to additional tutorials, strategies, or community forums where players can share their experiences and learn from others.

## Edge Cases

1. If a player fails to recover from the FUBAR moment, consider offering an option to restart the game with a modified setup to encourage continued engagement.
2. For advanced players, provide additional challenges or options for customization to keep them engaged and challenged.
3. Ensure that the game is accessible to players of all skill levels by offering adjustable difficulty settings and clear explanations of complex concepts.
