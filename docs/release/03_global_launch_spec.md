# Point Zero One Digital - Global Launch Specification (Season 1)

## Overview

This document outlines the specifications for the Global Launch of Season 1, encompassing a daily challenge, creator/influencer toolkits, referral codes, community leagues, and an operational model emphasizing "launch is a season, not a date." Additionally, a Day 1 communications calendar is provided.

## Non-Negotiables

1. **Daily Challenge**: A new challenge will be presented to players each day, encouraging active participation and fostering competition.
2. **Creator/Influencer Toolkits**: Provide creators and influencers with the necessary tools to engage their audiences effectively and promote the game.
3. **Referral Codes**: Implement a system for users to invite friends and earn rewards or benefits.
4. **Community Leagues**: Organize leagues within the community to facilitate friendly competition, collaboration, and social interaction.
5. **"Launch is a Season, Not a Date" Operational Model**: Adopt an approach that emphasizes ongoing engagement and updates rather than focusing solely on the initial release date.
6. **Day 1 Communications Calendar**: Plan and execute a comprehensive communications strategy for the game's launch day to maximize visibility and user acquisition.

## Implementation Spec

### Daily Challenge

- Design a new challenge each day, ensuring variety and increasing difficulty over time.
- Integrate leaderboards to showcase top performers and foster competition.
- Reward participants with in-game items or currency.

### Creator/Influencer Toolkits

- Develop a suite of tools tailored for creators and influencers, including promotional materials, exclusive content, and APIs for integration into their channels.
- Offer incentives for promoting the game, such as early access to new features or exclusive in-game items.

### Referral Codes

- Implement a system that allows users to invite friends using unique referral codes.
- Reward both the inviter and the invited user with in-game benefits upon successful referral.

### Community Leagues

- Organize leagues based on skill level, platform, or region to encourage friendly competition and collaboration among players.
- Provide rewards for league winners, such as exclusive in-game items or early access to new content.

### "Launch is a Season, Not a Date" Operational Model

- Continuously update the game with new challenges, features, and content to keep users engaged throughout the season.
- Monitor user feedback and analytics to inform updates and improvements.

### Day 1 Communications Calendar

- Plan a series of announcements, social media posts, emails, and other communications leading up to and on launch day.
- Coordinate with creators and influencers to amplify the launch message and attract new users.

## Edge Cases

- In the event of technical issues or unforeseen circumstances, have contingency plans in place to minimize disruption and maintain user trust.
- Monitor user feedback and be prepared to adapt strategies as needed based on community response and engagement levels.```
